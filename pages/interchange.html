There are three fees collected by various parties each time you swipe your credit card:

  • interchange fee
  • scheme fee
  • acquirer fee

The interchange fee goes to the issuing bank, which is the bank that issued the card to the customer. For example, when somebody uses a "Chase Sapphire Preferred" card, the interchange fee goes to J.P. Morgan Chase.

Even though Visa does not keep the interchange fee, it has the power to set it. Visa decides what the interchange fee is and the fee is different across its products (Visa, Visa Signature, Visa Infinite, etc.), merchant categories (Retail, Travel, Hospitality, etc.), and other factors. Visa updates these rates several times a year and as of the time of writing the latest rates can be seen here.

So what does Visa collect for itself? The scheme fee. This fee is not revealed publicly, but I've heard on podcasts that it is in the range of 0.2%-0.3% of the transaction.
